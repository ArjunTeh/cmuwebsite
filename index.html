Hey! I'm a final-year PhD student at Carnegie Mellon University, co-advised by Matt O'Toole and Ioannis Gkioulekas. My research focuses on computational methods for designing geometric optics, where I combine differentiable rendering with Markov chain Monte Carlo optimization techniques. I build differentiable simulators that solve both reconstruction problems and design optimization tasks.

Before starting my PhD, I worked as a software engineer at Magic Leap and studied Computer Engineering at the University of Texas at Austin.
